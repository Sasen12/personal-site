Balancing school, side projects, and actually having a life is hard. Here's what works for me.

## The Pomodoro + Flow Hybrid

I don't stick to strict Pomodoro timers. Instead I use the Pomodoro method to *start* — I commit to 25 minutes of focused work with my phone in another room. Once I'm in the zone, I ignore the timer and keep going. If I'm not in the zone after 25 minutes, I take a break and try something else.

## Project Journal

Every time I sit down to work on a project, I write one line about what I plan to do. At the end of the session I write one line about what actually happened. That's it — no elaborate bullet journal system.

This has been surprisingly effective. It stops me from opening the same file and staring at it for an hour, and it gives me a record of progress to look back on.

## Tools I Rely On

- **Obsidian** — all notes, project planning, and the journal above
- **iTerm2 + tmux** — terminal sessions that survive laptop closures
- **GitHub CLI** — creating repos and PRs without leaving the terminal
- **Tidal** — instrumental music for focus loops

## The Hardest Lesson

The most important thing I've learned: done is better than perfect. I've abandoned more projects because I was trying to make them perfect than because they were too hard. Shipping something imperfect beats planning something flawless.

This site itself is proof — it's live and I'm still adding to it every week.
