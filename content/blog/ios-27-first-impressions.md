I've been running the iOS 27 beta since Monday. Here's the thing nobody's talking about — it's not the flashy AI features that make this release. It's the fact that Apple Intelligence has stopped feeling like a separate thing you opt into and started feeling like iOS itself.

## The Standout Changes

**Notification Intelligence** is quietly the most impactful feature. My notification center went from a firehose of distraction to an actual triage system. The on-device model learns which notifications I swipe away without reading and starts suppressing similar ones. It's like having a really good PA spam-filtering my lockscreen.

The Photos app got another redesign, but this one actually makes sense. The new memory movies feature uses the LLM to let me type things like "show me all the times we went hiking last summer" and it compiles a coherent edit with music and transitions in about four seconds. It's genuinely good enough that I stopped using Luma for quick edits.

## What I'm Skeptical About

The new Live Activities API is powerful but I worry about battery life. I've already seen some third-party widgets chewing through background activity. Apple needs to police this aggressively, because if my battery tanks, I don't care how smart the assistant is.

Also, the Control Center redesign is going to confuse my mom. I love the customization (finally, automations as buttons!), but there's a learning curve. I wish Apple had shipped a guided setup for it.

## Verdict

iOS 27 feels like the first version of iOS that was designed *with* AI from the ground up, rather than having AI bolted on afterward. If the battery life holds up through the beta cycle, this will be a remarkably solid release.
