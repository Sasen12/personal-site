I'll start with a confession: I bought the original Vision Pro, used it intensely for three weeks, and then it spent six months gathering dust on my desk. The hardware was incredible. The software felt like a series of tech demos masquerading as an OS. visionOS 27 feels like the first release that understands what this device should actually be.

## The Shift

Apple is leaning hard into "spatial computer" rather than "VR headset with some apps." The new workspace features let me pin up to 24 app windows in physical space, and they persist between sessions. I set up my office layout once — Xcode front and center, Safari to the right, Terminal floating above my desk, Discord tucked to the left — and it just stays there. Walking into my office and seeing my digital workspace exactly where I left it is genuinely magical.

## What's New

**Spatial Persona 2.0** is a massive leap. Eye contact actually works now — the avatars look *at* you instead of slightly above your left shoulder. The machine learning model running on the R2 chip tracks your facial expressions with unsettling accuracy. I had a FaceTime call with a colleague on the other coast and forgot I was wearing a headset for about 20 minutes.

The new **Mac Virtual Display** supports up to 8K and three virtual monitors. I've been using it as my primary development setup for three days and the latency is imperceptible. This alone justifies the price tag for me.

## The Elephant in the Room

It's still expensive. It's still heavy after about two hours. But for the first time, I don't feel like I bought a developer kit. visionOS 27 is legitimately useful software running on hardware that finally has enough polish. I think this is the point where the Vision Pro stops being a curiosity and starts being a tool.
