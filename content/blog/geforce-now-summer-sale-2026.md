## Cloud Gaming Finally Feels Like a Real Product

GeForce NOW is running a summer sale — up to $70 off the annual Ultimate membership, bringing it down to something like $200/year. I've been a subscriber for two years now, and this is the first time I feel like the value proposition genuinely works.

A few years ago, cloud gaming was a laggy mess with a library of games nobody wanted. The GeForce NOW model — stream games you already own from Steam/Epic/Xbox — was always the right idea, but the execution lagged. Latency was noticeable, queues were long, and the free tier was basically unusable.

Fast forward to 2026. With the Vera Rubin-powered RTX SuperPods in their data centers, the streaming quality is shockingly good. I played Elden Ring on a MacBook Air over Wi-Fi 7 at 1440p 120fps and genuinely forgot I was streaming. The adaptive bitrate kicks in seamlessly, and Reflex 3.0 in the cloud reduces perceived latency to within spitting distance of local hardware.

The sale is clearly a push to lock in subscribers before the holiday quarter, but I'm not mad at it. $200/year for "RTX 7090-class" hardware (whatever they call it) in the cloud, with no hardware upgrades, no heat management, no driver issues — that's a compelling deal for someone like me who travels constantly.

My one gripe: the library still has gaps. There's a nonzero number of major publishers who refuse to license their games for cloud streaming. That's the next frontier for this service.
