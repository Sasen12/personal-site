Google dropped Gemini Spark at I/O and I've been thinking about it all week. A persistent, always-on AI agent that lives in your Google account, remembers everything, and can act on your behalf 24/7. No, it's not just a rebranded Assistant.

## What Makes It Different

Spark isn't a chatbot you talk to and forget. It's an agent with:

- **Persistent memory** — remembers context across days and devices
- **Proactive actions** — it doesn't wait for you to ask
- **Cross-app orchestration** — moves between Gmail, Drive, Calendar, Photos seamlessly

Google showed it noticing a flight confirmation in Gmail, checking calendar conflicts, adding the event, and texting my partner about pickup time — all without a single prompt from the user.

## The Privacy Question

Of course, this creeps people out. An AI that watches everything? Google's answer is "on-device processing where possible" and something they're calling *Spark Tiers* — your data stays local for personal stuff and only touches cloud for cross-device actions.

I'm cautiously optimistic. The utility of a true personal agent is enormous — especially for ADHD-having developers like me who live in fifteen tabs and still miss appointments. But Google's trust deficit is real. They need to nail transparency here.

This is the future of computing. Whether that's comforting or terrifying depends entirely on who controls the agent.
