## My Takeaway From This Year's Build

Microsoft Build 2026 was, predictably, an AI parade. But unlike last year where everything felt rushed and bolted-on, this year's announcements actually felt… cohesive? Let me break down what stood out.

### The big announcements

- **Copilot SDK v2** — Custom agents can now hook into the entire Microsoft 365 graph. Calendar, email, Teams, SharePoint — it's all fair game. Privacy implications aside, the developer experience is genuinely slick.
- **Azure AI Foundry** — A unified platform for fine-tuning, evaluating, and deploying models. Supports OpenAI, Llama, Mistral, and Anthropic models side by side. No lock-in pitch, which I respect.
- **Windows AI Dev Kit** — Local NPU programming finally has decent tooling. ONNX Runtime got a massive upgrade, and the new profiling tools actually work.

### What bothered me

The elephant in the room: **Copilot licensing**. Every demo assumed you're on enterprise E5 plans. The pricing for embedding Copilot into your own SaaS product via the new "Copilot Connect" program starts at a point that'll make indie developers wince.

Also, the emphasis on "AI agents managing your inbox and scheduling" feels dystopian when you think about it for more than five seconds. Do we really need an AI to accept meeting invites on our behalf? Who's accountable when the agent double-books?

### Bottom line

Microsoft is betting the company on AI agents as the next OS-level paradigm. From a platform play perspective, it's brilliant. From a "do I want this" perspective — I'm cautiously optimistic but keeping my skepticism handy. The tools for developers are genuinely better. The vision for users still needs work.
