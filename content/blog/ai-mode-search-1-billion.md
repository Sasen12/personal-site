Google announced that AI Mode in Search crossed 1 billion monthly active users. That's a number so large it almost loses meaning — but let's sit with it for a second. A fundamentally new way of interacting with the internet reached a billion people in under a year.

## What Changed

AI Mode isn't just a chatbot overlay on search results. It's a full rethinking of how queries work:

- **Conversational follow-ups** — ask "best hiking trails near me" then "which ones are dog-friendly?" naturally
- **Synthetic answers** — the AI synthesizes info from multiple sources instead of just ranking links
- **Source transparency** — every claim links back to a specific page

## The Good

For complex queries — "plan a 3-day trip to Tokyo under $1500" — AI Mode is genuinely better than ten blue links. The synthesis saves time. I use it daily for research and troubleshooting code.

## The Concern

A billion people means a billion opportunities for the model to get things subtly wrong. Google's been burned by AI hallucinations before. The stakes are higher now because people *trust* search results in a way they don't trust chatbots.

I also worry about the web's traffic economy. If AI Mode answers everything inline, who visits the actual sites? Google says they'll prioritize source attribution, but attribution doesn't pay hosting bills.

Search is better now, but the incentives are getting weird. That's the tradeoff we're all making.
