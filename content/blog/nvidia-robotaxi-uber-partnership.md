## Two Giants, One Self-Driving Bet

When I saw the press release about NVIDIA partnering with Uber on robotaxis, my first thought was: why didn't this happen sooner?

Uber famously burned billions trying to build self-driving tech in-house, then offloaded ATG to Aurora in 2020. They've been quiet on autonomy ever since. Meanwhile NVIDIA has been quietly building the full autonomous vehicle stack — from Orin/Thor SoCs to Drive Sim to the mapping pipeline. This partnership makes terrifyingly good sense.

Uber brings the hardest part of the problem: real-world logistics, driver network effects, regulatory relationships in 70+ countries, and a mountain of historical routing data. NVIDIA brings the compute platform and the AI expertise. It's a marriage of the operational and the technological.

My real take: this is about data moats. Robotaxi success isn't just about perception models — it's about handling edge cases at scale. Uber has been handling edge cases in ride-hailing for over a decade. NVIDIA has the simulation chops to test billions of miles virtually. Combined, they can solve the long-tail problem faster than Waymo or Tesla.

The skeptic in me worries about integration complexity. NVIDIA's Drive platform is sophisticated, and Uber's engineering culture is very different. But if they pull this off, 2028 could look very different for urban transportation.
