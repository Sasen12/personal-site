## Top 10 Foundries Grew 3.7% QoQ — And That's Actually a Big Deal

The latest foundry revenue numbers are out: top 10 foundries grew 3.7% quarter-over-quarter, driven by AI HPC and consumer inventory builds. In a world where "growth" is usually 1-2% for mature industries, 3.7% QoQ annualizes to nearly 16% YoY. That's bonkers for a capital-intensive manufacturing sector.

### Who's driving this?

TSMC is the obvious king — AI accelerators are soaking up 3nm and 5nm capacity like there's no tomorrow. But the interesting story is the tail:

- **Samsung Foundry** — finally gaining traction with their SF2 (2nm GAA) process winning some high-profile AI chip designs
- **SMIC** — quietly shipping more than people realize, albeit at older nodes, feeding the IoT and consumer chip recovery
- **UMC & GlobalFoundries** — mature nodes are surprisingly hot right now. Not everything needs 3nm. Power management ICs, WiFi chips, microcontrollers — these are all on 28nm-12nm and demand is surging

### The consumer inventory story

We've been hearing about "inventory correction" for two years. That's ending. Consumer electronics demand is back — phones, PCs, gaming consoles, IoT. Companies that over-corrected during the downturn are now scrambling to restock. That's providing a nice floor under foundry utilization rates even as AI HPC drives the premium segment.

### My take

The semiconductor industry has always been cyclical. But this cycle feels different. AI demand isn't a wave — it's a permanent step-function increase in compute demand. Combined with the consumer recovery, I think we're looking at sustained growth for at least another 2-3 years. The cyclical dip everybody keeps predicting might not come this time.

The foundry business is boring, capital-intensive, and easy to ignore. But it's the foundation everything else runs on. These numbers tell me the foundation is solid.
